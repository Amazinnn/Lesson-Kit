# ICAP Framework

## Core Claim

Student engagement falls into four modes — Interactive, Constructive, Active, Passive — predicting increasingly deeper learning outcomes in that order (I > C > A > P). The key insight is that physical engagement (doing something) does not guarantee cognitive engagement (thinking about it).

## Key Research

Chi & Wylie (2014) presented the ICAP framework with supporting evidence from hundreds of studies. Chi received the 2023 Yidan Prize for Education Research for this work. Recent 2024 evidence (Qian, Hong & Chi, IJSTEM) shows that students watching dialog videos (two tutors discussing) learned more than those watching monolog lectures — because dialog videos encouraged Constructive engagement.

## Effect Size & Confidence

- Interactive > Constructive: d = 0.40–0.70 (medium confidence — highly context-dependent)
- Constructive > Active: d = 0.50–0.80 (medium-high confidence)
- Active > Passive: d = 0.60–1.00 (high confidence)
- The full I>C>A>P hierarchy is well-supported across hundreds of studies.

## Boundary Conditions

- Interactive learning requires genuine co-construction of knowledge — not just talking in groups.
- Not all content benefits from higher engagement modes: initial exposure (速览) may legitimately be Passive-to-Active.
- Implementation quality matters more than mode: a poorly designed Interactive activity can be worse than well-designed Constructive one.

## Practical Implications

- 速览 MCQs move students from Passive (reading) to Active (selecting an answer) — a meaningful upgrade.
- The "return to source" requirement pushes toward Constructive engagement (comparing source with MCQ answer).
- For later stages, design Constructive activities (self-explanation, summarizing) and Interactive activities (peer discussion, collaborative problem-solving).
- The spectrum should inform progression: 速览 (A) → 课程 (C) → 习题 (C/I) → 复习 (I).

## Connection to Other Theories

- **Testing Effect** (see fundamentals/testing-effect.md): Answering an MCQ is Active engagement; explaining WHY is Constructive.
- **Feedback** (see fundamentals/feedback-learning.md): The quality of feedback determines whether an Active activity becomes Constructive.
