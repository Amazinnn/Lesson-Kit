# Structuring Practice & Exercises

## 一句话定位

练习不是"检验学会了没有"，而是"让学会的过程发生"——每道题都是一次编码强化。

## Related Theories

- **Retrieval Practice** (see fundamentals/testing-effect.md): The act of retrieving knowledge strengthens neural pathways. Practice IS learning, not just evidence of learning.
- **Spacing Effect** (see fundamentals/spacing-effect.md): Distributed practice produces dramatically better retention than massed practice. A concept revisited after a gap is better encoded.
- **Interleaving**: Mixing different problem types forces discrimination and selection — building transferable knowledge. Blocked practice (all one type) feels effective but produces fragile knowledge.
- **Deliberate Practice** (Ericsson): Practice must be focused, goal-directed, at the edge of current ability, with immediate feedback. Generic "do more problems" is not deliberate practice.

## Teaching Application

- For 速览 (Stage 1): MCQs are sufficient. They provide retrieval practice + feedback in minimal time.
- For later stages: transition from recognition (MCQ) to recall (short answer) to application (problem solving).
- The spacing principle suggests: if a concept appears in section A and again in section C, the second appearance serves as spaced retrieval. The through-line perspective naturally creates this effect.
- Recommended progression: 速览 (low-stakes MCQ) → 课程 (explanation + examples) → 习题 (applied + interleaved) → 复习 (spaced, cumulative).

## Aha Moment

> Most students think "I did 50 problems so I'm prepared." Research says: "I retrieved the correct approach at the right moment of difficulty" is what matters. Problem count matters far less than retrieval quality and spacing.
