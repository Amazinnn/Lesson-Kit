# Choosing Through-Line Perspectives

## 一句话定位

贯穿视角不是"这个知识点的总结"，而是"选择一条将在全章反复出现的线索，第一次遇见时就做上标记"。

## Related Theories

- **Advance Organizer** (Ausubel, 1960): Presenting a high-level organizing structure before detailed content improves comprehension and retention. The through-line is a recurring advance organizer.
- **Elaboration Theory** (Reigeluth, 1999): Instruction should start with a single, simple organizing idea (the "epitome"), then elaborate with increasing complexity. Through-line perspectives are the epitome.
- **Structural Scaffolding**: Recurring patterns provide cognitive scaffolding — students learn to ask "where have I seen this pattern before?" which builds transfer.

## Teaching Application

- The through-line should be a pattern or operation that appears at least 3 times across the chapter.
- It should be specific enough to be recognizable, general enough to recur naturally.
- Good candidates: "every transformation theorem replaces a boundary integral with a region integral" (recurring_operation); "each new concept increases dimension by one" (dimension_evolution).
- Bad candidates: generic advice like "pay attention to formulas" or "read carefully."
- The through-line is stated in the first knowledge point where it appears, then referenced implicitly in later KPs — not repeated explicitly.

## Aha Moment

> A well-chosen through-line makes later KPs feel familiar ("oh, it's that pattern again"). A poorly chosen one feels like a gimmick. The difference is whether the pattern genuinely recurs in the source or is imposed from outside.
