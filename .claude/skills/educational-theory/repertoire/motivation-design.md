# Motivating Learners

## 一句话定位

学习动机不是学生自带的东西，而是教学设计可以培养的东西——当学生知道"为什么要学"并且相信自己"能学会"时，动机自然产生。

## Related Theories

- **Expectancy-Value Theory** (Eccles & Wigfield): Motivation = Expectancy of success × Perceived value of the task. Both must be present.
- **Self-Determination Theory** (Deci & Ryan): Intrinsic motivation requires three needs: autonomy (I choose to learn), competence (I can do this), and relatedness (this connects to my world).
- **Growth Mindset** (Dweck): Students who believe intelligence is malleable persist longer and achieve more than those who see it as fixed.

## Teaching Application

- Value: The chapter overview and through-line perspective show WHY this content matters (answering "why am I reading this?").
- Expectancy: The 速览's low-stakes format (MCQ/true-false, no calculation) signals "you can do this." Start easy, build confidence.
- Competence: Granular knowledge points (3-8 min each) create frequent small successes — each answered MCQ is a competence signal.
- Autonomy: The source-reading format ("go read pages X-Y") puts the student in control. The 速览 is guidance, not instruction.
- Growth mindset: Framing wrong answers as "useful information about what to re-read" rather than "failure" reinforces effort-based learning.

## Aha Moment

> A 速览 that is too easy kills motivation (no challenge, no growth signal). A 速览 that is too hard kills motivation (no success, helplessness). The sweet spot — "desirable difficulty" — is where effort is required but success is achievable.

## In-workflow Application

- `chapter overview (章首)`: Establishes value and provides orientation
- `quick_absorption`: The through-line accent signals "there's a pattern here worth spotting"
- `MCQ design`: Low-stakes retrieval builds competence signals
- `knowledge granularity`: 3-8 min per KP keeps tasks achievable
