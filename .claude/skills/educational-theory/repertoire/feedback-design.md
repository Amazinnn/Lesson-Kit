# Designing Feedback

## 一句话定位

反馈不是"告诉学生对错"，而是"帮学生发现自己的盲区，并且知道下一步该看哪里"。

## Related Theories

- **Feedback Types** (Hattie & Timperley, 2007; see fundamentals/feedback-learning.md): Four levels — task, process, self-regulation, self. Task feedback (is this right?) is the minimum; process feedback (how to improve) is more powerful.
- **Error-Driven Learning**: The brain updates its mental models when prediction errors occur. A wrong answer that surprises + a clear explanation of WHY it's wrong = optimal learning event.
- **Formative Assessment**: Feedback is most effective when it's immediate, specific, and actionable. The 速览's section-end answers provide immediate verification.

## Teaching Application

- The answer explanation must be ONE sentence — just enough to clarify why the correct answer is correct, not a full re-explanation.
- Do NOT explain why each wrong option is wrong in the answer key. The student should figure that out by returning to the source.
- If the student chose the wrong answer, the one-sentence explanation should help them locate their error: "Q1 is B because the definition specifies closed curves — A describes open curves."
- Feedback timing: immediate (section-end answers) is appropriate for 速览. For later stages, delayed feedback can enhance retention.

## Aha Moment

> The best feedback doesn't answer "what's the right answer" — it answers "why was my answer wrong and where do I look to correct my understanding." The difference is between giving a fish and teaching fishing.
