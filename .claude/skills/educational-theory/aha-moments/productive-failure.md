# Productive Failure

Letting students struggle with a problem BEFORE being taught the solution produces deeper learning than direct instruction alone — even when (especially when) they fail to solve it.

## Why This Matters

It's counterintuitive: teaching the solution first feels more efficient. But the struggle activates prior knowledge, reveals gaps, and primes the brain to encode the subsequent explanation more deeply. The failure is productive because it creates a cognitive "hook" for the correct solution.

## Source

Kapur (2008). Productive failure. *Cognition and Instruction*, 26(3), 379–424.

## How It Connects

A powerful form of **desirable difficulty** (see fundamentals/desirable-difficulties.md). Related to the **generation effect** (see aha-moments/generation-effect.md). Also connects to why MCQ pretesting — attempting questions before reading — improves learning.
