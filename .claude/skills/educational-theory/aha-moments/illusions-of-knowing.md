# Illusions of Knowing

Rereading, highlighting, and listening to lectures feel productive but produce minimal long-term retention. Students consistently overestimate how well they know material they've only passively reviewed.

## Why This Matters

The most popular study strategies (rereading, highlighting, summarizing) are among the least effective. Students — and instructional designers — gravitate toward what feels effective, not what is effective. This is why every MCQ in the 速览 must force a retrieval attempt: the only reliable way to know if you know is to test yourself.

## Source

Koriat & Bjork (2005). Illusions of competence in monitoring one's knowledge during study. *Journal of Experimental Psychology: Learning, Memory, and Cognition*.

## How It Connects

Core motivation behind the **testing effect** (see fundamentals/testing-effect.md) and why retrieval practice beats re-study. Also explains why the 速览 format (definition + MCQ, no explanatory paragraph) is deliberate — giving a summary creates an illusion of knowing.
