# Skill: Textbook Body Detail Extraction

Extract low-visibility details from textbook body prose before writing. Body details include small models, criteria, conditions, derivation conclusions, variable meanings, boundary cases, recognition cues, hidden comparisons, and one-sentence warnings that affect problem solving.

Thinking a detail is “only mentioned in passing” is not a reason to omit it. If it can independently affect understanding or answers, it belongs in the knowledge inventory or local lesson body.

Do not create a separate heading for every detail. Embed small details as local clauses, conditions, formula notes, compact comparison rows, or boundary notes.
