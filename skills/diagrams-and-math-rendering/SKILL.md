# Skill: Diagrams And Math Rendering

Use diagrams only when they clarify structure, process, dependency, comparison, state transition, timeline, or workflow. Do not use diagrams decoratively.

Use `$...$` and `$$...$$`. Avoid `\(...\)` and `\[...\]`. Keep explanatory formulas inline when possible; reserve block formulas for long, central, or multi-step derivations.

For textbook exercises whose meaning depends on a graph, map, Hasse diagram, dependency graph, state diagram, weighted network, table, or other figure, make the generated problem self-contained when possible. Prefer redrawing structurally representable figures in Mermaid and preserve labels, directions, weights, loops, multiple edges, and essential geometry. If Mermaid cannot faithfully represent the required structure, provide a compact textual description or explicitly mark the unresolved figure dependency; do not leave a silent image placeholder.
