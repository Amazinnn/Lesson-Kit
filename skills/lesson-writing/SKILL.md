# Skill: Lesson Writing

Write high-density but readable lesson notes. Explain definitions, meanings, why concepts are introduced, conditions, boundaries, formulas, models, derivation conclusions, process traces, representation changes, code/pseudocode when useful, and applications.

Use examples sparingly and functionally. A typical example should cover 2–3 related knowledge points; use one example for a large integrated block when better. Do not attach a problem to every detail.

Keep small details inside the relevant knowledge block. Do not convert the lesson into a problem collection, wrong-answer notebook, or formalistic type list.
